The **Signposting Toolkit** is a web-based system designed to support GP reception and care-navigation teams in making safe, consistent decisions about where patients should be directed. Built within an NHS primary care setting, the toolkit brings structure, clarity, and repeatability to one of the busiest and most pressured parts of general practice: **front-door triage**.

---

## Why it exists

Reception and care-navigation teams make hundreds of triage decisions every day.  

But without a shared, up-to-date reference:

- staff rely on memory or wording from past notes  

- decisions vary between individuals  

- high-risk symptoms can be missed  

- local processes aren't always followed consistently  

- onboarding new receptionists takes longer than it should  

The Signposting Toolkit solves this by providing **a single, standardised, clinically-reviewed source of truth** for patient signposting.

## Core Components

### 1. Symptom Library

A structured library of 200+ base symptoms covering common and urgent primary care presentations.

Practices can:

- override the base text  

- create additional custom symptoms  

- configure high-risk flags  

- adjust instructions for local pathways  

- include advice for children under/over 5 and adults  

Every symptom has a clear, readable instruction set designed for non-clinical staff.

---

### 2. High-Risk Flags & Safety Highlighting

The Toolkit automatically highlights:

- red / urgent indicators  

- amber caution wording  

- pink / purple safeguarding phrases  

- common pharmacy-first opportunities  

- keywords configured by the practice  

This draws staff attention to important red flags and ensures nothing vital is overlooked.

---

### 3. AI-Assisted Clarity Tools

AI features are designed to **support clarity**, not decision-making.

Tools include:

- **AI Instruction Editor** â€” rewrites receptionist instructions to be clearer and easier to read  

- **AI Suggested Questions** â€” practical follow-up questions staff can ask callers to better understand the presentation  

All AI-generated content requires **mandatory clinical review** before it goes live.

---

### 4. Appointment Directory

A structured list of:

- appointment types  

- local care teams  

- practice-specific slots  

- external services (e.g. pharmacies, PCN teams)  

This helps receptionists quickly see which service or person deals with which symptoms.

---

### 5. Clinical Governance Workflow

Each symptom moves through:

1. **Draft**  

2. **Submitted for Review**  

3. **Approved**  

This ensures that all guidance is clinically validated and reflects up-to-date local practice.  

The toolkit also supports periodic review cycles.

---

### 6. Multi-Surgery / Multi-Site Support

The Toolkit supports:

- practices with multiple sites  

- PCNs with shared workflows  

- configurations that differ between surgeries  

- separate approval flows per organisation  

Central updates can be applied without disrupting local customisations.

---

### 7. Role-Based Access Control

Different users see different tools:

- **Superusers** â€” can manage global settings  

- **Surgery Admins** â€” manage symptoms, local overrides, governance workflow  

- **Standard Users** â€” reception/care-navigation staff using the live symptom library  

---

### 8. Practice Handbook

An internal knowledge base for storing surgery-specific guidance pages, operational procedures, and staff information. Practices can create structured content with categories, role cards, and a pinned operational panel. Supports category visibility controls and per-item editing permissions to share sensitive guidance safely.

---

### 9. Workflow Guidance

Visual workflow diagrams to guide staff through common internal processes such as processing documents, handling test results, and managing referrals. Includes global default workflows with per-surgery customisation, and operates under the same draft/approved governance lifecycle as symptom content.

## What it is *not*

- Not a clinical decision-support system  

- Not a diagnostic tool  

- Not a replacement for clinical review  

- Not designed for patient-facing use  

It is a **clarity tool** that supports frontline staff and strengthens governance.
